FAQs

Occupational Therapy is a treatment approach that focuses on functioning. In order to enable clients to be able to do what they want to do, (and often need to do), OTs use approaches targetted at physical, cognitive, and mental health, in addition to environemntal factors that may be impacting the person.

Using this individualized approach, OTs work with clients to achieve functional goals that are unique to each person. This may look quite different depending on the practice setting (e.g., hospitals, schools, clinics, or long term care to name a few).

OTs typically work in a variety of settings AND with people of all ages! Check out our “Services” page to learn how a mental health OT can support you.

An occupational therapist (OT), psychotherapist, psychologist, and a coach can all support people with ADHD effectively!

OTs typically use hollistic approaches considering all aspects of health and the environment, in relation to functional goals.

Psychotherapists, often focus primarily on mental health and reducing the impact of symptoms on an individual’s wellbeing.

Psychologists use a similar approach to psychotherapists, however they can also diagnose mental health conditions (such as ADHD!).

Coach’s typically work with people to invoke behaviour changes towards the individual’s desired goals. Often, psychotherapists and OTs will use coaching techniques in their practice.

OTs, psychotherapists, and psychologists are regulated healthcare providers (in Canada) and require specific education and experience to use these titles. These providers will be registered with their regulatory bodies provincially.
